The Beighton Score is a standardized screening tool used to assess generalized joint hypermobility. It evaluates flexibility at specific joints using a nine-point scale. While useful for screening, it does not capture pain, instability, or systemic symptoms and may underestimate hypermobility in some individuals, especially adults or those with localized joint involvement.
