---
title: Why is hEDS (hypermobile Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome) so hard to diagnose?
embed: null

description: ''
seoOverrides: null
---

hEDS is diagnosed clinically because there is no confirmed genetic test. Diagnosis requires recognizing a pattern of symptoms, joint hypermobility, and associated features while excluding other connective tissue disorders. Symptoms often overlap with other conditions, which can delay recognition and lead to misdiagnosis.
