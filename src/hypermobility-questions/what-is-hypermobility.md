Not everyone who is hypermobile has a disorder. Hypermobility simply means that some joints move beyond the typical range of motion. For some people, this is harmless. For others, it can be associated with pain, injuries, fatigue, dysautonomia, gastrointestinal symptoms, and connective tissue disorders such as the Ehlers-Danlos syndromes.
