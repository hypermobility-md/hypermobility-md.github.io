If you think you are hypermobile, the first step is to learn whether your joint hypermobility is simply a normal trait or part of a medical condition such as **Hypermobility Spectrum Disorder (HSD)** or **hypermobile Ehlers-Danlos syndrome (hEDS)**.

If your hypermobility is accompanied by symptoms such as chronic pain, joint instability, frequent sprains or dislocations, fatigue, dizziness, gastrointestinal problems, or other unexplained symptoms, schedule an evaluation with a healthcare professional who has experience diagnosing hypermobility disorders. An accurate diagnosis can help identify the underlying cause of your symptoms and guide appropriate treatment.

In the meantime, avoid repeatedly stretching already flexible joints or performing activities that cause pain or instability. Instead, focus on maintaining overall fitness, protecting your joints, and building strength under the guidance of a qualified healthcare professional. Early recognition and appropriate management can reduce injuries, improve function, and help you maintain an active, healthy lifestyle.
