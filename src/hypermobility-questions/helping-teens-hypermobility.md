Supporting a teen with a hypermobility disorder involves several important strategies:

- **Stay calm and be their advocate** — Your child will mirror your emotional state. Remaining steady helps them feel secure
- **Help them build resilience** — Encourage self-advocacy, positive self-talk, and avoiding comparison with peers
- **Set realistic goals** — Use SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) to create manageable milestones
- **Organize medical records** — Keep a one-page medical summary to bring to appointments
- **Explore resources** — Books like _Disjointed_ and _Conquering Your Child's Chronic Pain_, plus the [Bendy Bodies Podcast](/podcast/), offer practical guidance

Many teens with hypermobility disorders go on to lead fulfilling, functional lives. Some symptoms, such as POTS, may even improve over time.
