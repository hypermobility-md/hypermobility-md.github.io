The 2017 International Classification for hEDS requires meeting **all three** of the following criteria:

1. **Generalized joint hypermobility** — Typically assessed using the Beighton score (a 9-point scale measuring mobility at specific joints)
2. **Two or more of the following features:**
   - Systemic manifestations of a connective tissue disorder (e.g., unusually soft skin, mild skin hyperextensibility, unexplained stretch marks)
   - Positive family history
   - Musculoskeletal complications (e.g., chronic pain, recurrent dislocations)
3. **Exclusion of other diagnoses** — Including other types of EDS (via genetic testing) and alternative diagnoses that may explain the symptoms
