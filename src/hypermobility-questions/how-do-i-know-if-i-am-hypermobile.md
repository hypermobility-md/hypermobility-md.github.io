Many people discover they are hypermobile because they can perform certain movements, such as touching their palms to the floor without bending their knees, bending their thumb to their forearm, or extending their elbows or knees beyond a typical range. The Beighton Score is a screening tool, but it is only one piece of the puzzle. Many people with symptomatic joint hypermobility **do not score highly** on the Beighton Score.
