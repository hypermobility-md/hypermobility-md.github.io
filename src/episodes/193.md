description: >-
  Spinal cerebral spinal fluid (CSF) leaks are significantly more common in
  those with connective tissue disorders than many realize, yet patients often
  suffer for years before finding the right diagnosis. 


  In this episode, neuroradiologist Dr. Andrew Callan joins Dr. Linda Bluestein
  and co-host Dr. Knight to discuss the complexities of CSF dynamics and the
  challenges of diagnosing leaks when routine imaging appears normal. Dr. Callan
  explains the different types of leaks, including the elusive CSF-venous
  fistula, and why common clinical assumptions, like the requirement of a low
  opening pressure, are often incorrect.


  From the Bern Score to the critical differences between Chiari malformations
  and positional brain sag, this conversation provides an essential roadmap for
  patients and clinicians navigating the "unseen" world of intracranial
  hypotension.
