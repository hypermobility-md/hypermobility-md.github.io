  There is no magic treatment for Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome, POTS, or mast cell
  activation disorders, but there is strategy. In this episode of Bendy Bodies,
  Dr. Linda Bluestein is joined by Dacre Knight, MD, founding Medical Director

  deeply thoughtful conversation about how complex chronic conditions should
  actually be treated.

  sequencing. What to address first when everything is flaring, how to balance
  short-term symptom relief with long-term sustainability, and why overtreatment
  can sometimes cause more harm than good. They discuss the pitfalls of siloed
  care, the insurance barriers that complicate physical therapy, and the
  importance of starting low, going slow, and minimizing treatment burden.


  The episode also tackles difficult but essential questions: What does "getting
  better" really mean in lifelong connective tissue disorders? How do clinicians
  avoid reactionary prescribing? And how can patients recognize the difference
  between a thoughtful care plan and a rushed one?
