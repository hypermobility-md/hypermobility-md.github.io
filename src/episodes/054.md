[04:08] **Dr. Linda Bluestein:** Sure. So mast cells are immune cells that are present in connective tissues all throughout the body. And what happens is mast cells can get activated and degranulate. [04:46] And then there's other conditions that mast cells are involved with. For example, we see mast cells in conditions like allergy, asthma, anaphylaxis, gastrointestinal disorders. We can see them involved in malignancies and cardiovascular disease.\
[05:02] The prevalence of mast cell disorders is really unknown.
