[05:17] If we think back to a little bit of the basics, hypermobility mostly has to do with how flexible your collagen is. [22:10] **Dr. Linda Bluestein:** Sure. Let's talk about tendinopathy. If you come back to the anatomy lesson, tendons connect muscle to bone — they're a thick, ropey structure. Tendinopathy is really just a broad term to mean a problem with the tendon.\
[22:31] Tendons have some blood supply depending on the tendon, and that's what's interesting — different tendons in our body are very different. For example, the Achilles tendon is unique in that the blood supply is in the sheath that surrounds the tendon. It's a very different tendon compared to something like a tendon in your elbow, and the way that these tendons get injured can be a little bit different.\
[23:00] There's tendonitis — and if you go back to Latin, -itis means inflammation. To literally have a tendonitis means there has to be inflammation, if we look at the true definition of that term. Unfortunately, that term gets thrown around a lot very incorrectly, even among the medical community. If everyone can just understand that -itis means inflammation — to truly have a tendonitis, you have to have inflammation. Inflammation really should be a short-lived, few-week issue. If pain in a tendon is really going beyond that, there's some question as to whether it truly is a tendonitis, or is there some other actual problem going on — not really that tendon but some other structure in that joint? Tendinosis usually starts a bit later in life — we do see it in older teenagers and in the twenties, but usually a little bit more in people who are older than that. Tendons in general don't have great blood flow, so they can be really stubborn problems.\
[24:57] What I was alluding to is: you have to have a decent blood supply to get inflammation. [25:32] Again, not impossible, and there are some good research studies on a couple of tendons like the elbow and even the Achilles, out of Australia, showing that with enough time pathology can reverse.
