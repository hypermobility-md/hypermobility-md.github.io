[47:43] **Lara Heimann:** For anyone — and really especially for anyone with hypermobility — I think the key is keeping the pelvis in a stable neutral position most of the time. [48:51] This is where it doesn't matter if you are hypermobile or not, but you need to be more vigilant when you are hypermobile, because the sacrum and ilium come together at the SI joint in a way that's really not the same as other bones. [49:38] One cue I give a lot: take your fingers one inch above the pubic bone in front, second and third finger pressed in toward the body, and imagine a string pulling straight back while firming. [50:27] I would say, especially in yoga or any movement practice — even walking around and standing — where is your weight distributed through your feet? It's also an area of shock absorption. We need that stability there to take the energy from the floor up through into the core.
